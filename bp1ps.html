PRESENTATION SKILLS

Instructor: Zara Haddad

Presentation Skills Course Modules

    Module 1: Introduction to Presentation Skills
    • What is a presentation?
    • Importance in academics and workplace
    • Types of presentations (informative, persuasive, instructional, etc.)
    • Common fears and overcoming them
    Module 2: Knowing Your Audience
    • Analyzing your audience
    • Customizing your Message
    • Cultural Sensitivity
    • Engaging different types of Listeners
    Module 3: Structuring Your Presentation
    • Creating an outline (Introduction, Body, Conclusion)
    • Crafting a strong opening and closing
    • Logical flow and transitions
    • Time management in presentations
    Module 4: Designing Effective Visual Aids
    • Slide design principles (simplicity, readability, consistency)
    • Tools: PowerPoint, Canva, Google Slides
    • Use of charts, graphs, images, and videos
    • Avoiding clutter and text overload
    Module 5: Verbal and Non-Verbal Communication
    • Voice modulation, tone, and clarity
    • Body language: gestures, eye contact, posture
    • Facial expressions and confidence
    • Avoiding filler words ("um", "like", "you know")
    Module 6: Handling Nerves and Building Confidence
    • Practice techniques and rehearsal
    • Breathing and relaxation strategies
    • Positive visualization
    • Dealing with stage fright
    Module 7: Interactive Presentation Techniques
    • Asking and answering questions
    • Audience participation techniques
    • Storytelling and analogies
    • Humor and personal anecdotes
    Module 8: Technical Setup and Preparedness
    • Microphone and projector basics
    • Managing handouts and clickers
    • Backup plans for tech failures
    • Online presentation setup (Zoom, Google Meet, etc.)
    Module 9: Presentation Practice & Peer Review
    • Practice sessions with peer feedback
    • Video recording and self-assessment
    • Constructive criticism and improvement
    Module 10: Final Presentation Project
    • Prepare and deliver a full-length presentation
    • Evaluation on content, delivery, visuals, and confidence
    • Instructor and peer feedback
